## Step 2: Brand Strategy & Positioning

- **Visual Approach:** I think the rebrand should be quietly disruptive. The collection speaks for itself — the visual identity should feel grounded and considered, not loud. Let the art and programming be the disruption, with the brand providing a calm, confident frame.

- **Creative Direction:** We have an internal manifesto called "Rooted Futures" that talks about reconnecting contemporary art with land, ecology, and indigenous knowledge systems. The key phrase is "art grows from the ground up."

- **Problem Solved:** We give people a space to slow down and encounter art that reconnects them with the natural world and cultural memory. In a city dominated by screens and speed, we offer presence.

- **Deeper Reason:** I grew up visiting museums that never showed artists who looked like me or told stories I recognized. Terra Verde exists to correct that — to build a living collection that reflects the full spectrum of human creative expression.

- **Customer Words:** Intimate, surprising, grounding.

- **Differentiators:** We're the only mid-size gallery in the region with a permanent collection focused on ecological art and indigenous futurism. Our residency program brings artists to work on-site with local communities, not in isolation.

- **Additional Thoughts:** We're also launching a community garden on the gallery grounds next spring — the brand needs to feel connected to that initiative without being "earthy cliché."

## Step 3: Brand Personality & Tone

- **Dinner Party Behavior:** They'd be the person who listens more than they talk, but when they speak everyone leans in. Warm, curious, asks unexpected questions. Definitely not the loudest in the room but somehow the most memorable.

- **Never Feel Like:** Corporate, academic, or exclusionary. Never like a velvet rope. Never cold or pretentious.

- **Energy/Mood:** Calm confidence with an undercurrent of quiet excitement. Like walking into a greenhouse — warm, alive, a little humid, full of things growing.

- **Soundtrack Genre:** Ambient electronic with organic textures. Think Nils Frahm meets Arca. Something that feels handmade but future-facing.

- **Artists/Diversification:** We're actively acquiring work from Firelei Baez, Otobong Nkanga, and Postcommodity. We're also starting a commissioning program with emerging artists from the Caribbean diaspora and First Nations communities across Canada.

---

## Step 4: Visual Identity & Style

- **Color Associations:** Deep greens, warm terracotta, off-white (not sterile white). Avoid: bright corporate blue, anything neon. We gravitate toward colors you'd find in nature — moss, clay, bark, stone.

- **Visual Style:** Somewhere in between. Not stark minimalism but not cluttered either. We want breathing room with rich moments. Think of a well-composed photograph — clean framing but the subject has depth and texture.

- **Admired Brands:** Aesop (their restraint and materiality), Ace Hotel (the cultural curation feel), and Serpentine Galleries (how they balance tradition with experimentation). Outside art: Patagonia's quiet activism.

- **Aesthetic Inspiration:** 1970s Brazilian modernist architecture (Lina Bo Bardi), Japanese wabi-sabi aesthetics, and the color palettes of Agnes Varda's films. There's a warmth and imperfection in all of these that we want to channel.

- **Decolonization Approach:** It should be woven into the DNA rather than stamped on top. We don't want a "decolonized" badge — we want the visual language itself to draw from non-Western design traditions naturally. Typography, layout, spatial thinking — all of it should reflect multiplicity without being performative.

---

## Step 5: Target Audience

- **Audience Description:** Our core audience is culturally engaged adults 25-55 — a mix of local Hamilton residents, university students and faculty from McMaster, visiting artists, and a growing national audience following our programming online. The younger segment is very online and values authenticity; the older segment cares about depth and substance. Both will respond positively to a brand that feels genuine rather than trendy.

- **Ideal Client:** A curious person who reads widely, travels intentionally, cares about where things come from. They buy fewer things but better things. They'd rather spend a Saturday at a gallery than a mall. They want to be challenged but not condescended to.

- **Desired Feeling:** A sense of discovery and belonging. Like they've found a place that was made for them but is open to everyone. Warm welcome, not cool exclusion.

- **Customer Frustrations:** They're tired of institutions that feel sterile, elitist, or disconnected from real communities. They're frustrated by galleries that claim diversity but only show it in programming, not in their permanent collection or leadership.

- **Avoid Customer Types:** We're not trying to attract people who treat gallery visits as social currency for Instagram. We want engaged visitors, not content tourists.

- **Brand Role:** A trusted guide — someone who opens doors to unfamiliar perspectives without being preachy. More like a knowledgeable friend than a lecturer.

## Step 6: Vision & Growth

- **5-Year Vision:** The leading mid-size gallery in Ontario for ecological and decolonial contemporary art. A model for how cultural institutions can be community-rooted and globally relevant simultaneously.

- **Expansion Plans:** Yes — we're adding a community residency wing, expanding our education program for local schools, and launching a seasonal publication (print + digital) that extends our curatorial voice beyond the gallery walls.

- **Dream Partnerships:** A joint exhibition with the Inhotim Institute in Brazil. A residency exchange with the Palais de Tokyo. And honestly, a collaboration with a Canadian fashion designer like Sid Neigum to bring art into wearable form.

- **Big Dream:** To be the gallery that changes how Canada thinks about who gets to define "Canadian art." To build a permanent collection that future generations look at and say "this is when things shifted."

- **Success Beyond Sales:** When our artists get major international shows. When local kids who visited on school trips come back as adults and bring their own children. When other institutions study our model. Attendance matters less than impact.

- **Long-term Focus:** Yes, the new mission is permanent. But the brand should have enough flexibility to encompass temporary projects, satellite events, and digital programming without feeling like it's stretching. Future-proof, not rigid.

- **Existing Collection:** The historical collection will be recontextualized, not hidden. We'll present it alongside new acquisitions with curatorial framing that asks questions rather than making declarations. The old and new should be in dialogue, not competition.

- **Competitors:** Hamilton Art Gallery is our closest peer locally. Nationally, we're compared to the Power Plant and the Contemporary Art Gallery in Vancouver. Our edge is our community integration and ecological focus — they operate more as traditional white-cube spaces.
